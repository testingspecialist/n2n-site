## Design Context

### Users
B2B industrial decision-makers in Latin America (directors, ops managers, procurement) evaluating whether N2N's "arquitectura comercial digital" approach fits their operation. They arrive skeptical — they've seen marketing-agency sites and SaaS pitches and are looking for a structural, technical counterpart to their operation. Content is in Spanish (Argentina). Viewing is mixed: desktop for long evaluation / framework reading, mobile for first-contact and WhatsApp handoff.

### Brand Personality
Technical · Precise · Operational. N2N reads like a structural report, not a sales pitch. The product itself is about filtering noise and representing operational reality with fidelity — the site must embody that discipline. Emotional goal: the buyer should feel "these people understand my operation," not "these people want to sell me something."

### Aesthetic Direction
- Structural, document-like, spec-sheet energy. Navy + white + restrained accents.
- Explicit anti-references: SaaS/Linear-clone dark gradients, marketing-agency hero flourishes, Bootstrap-era B2B template grids, consumer ecommerce polish.
- Theme: light (document/report) with select dark-navy sections for emphasis. No dark-mode-with-glow aesthetic.
- Typography should feel editorial/technical, not generic. Current Outfit + DM Sans + Rajdhani reads as reflex-pick territory and should be audited.

### Design Principles
1. **Structure over decoration** — every element earns its place by conveying operational meaning. Decorative flourishes are anti-brand.
2. **Restraint is the point** — the product filters noise; the interface must too. Sparse rhythm, dense content where it matters.
3. **Technical credibility** — specification-sheet precision: clear hierarchy, no hero metrics for the sake of it, no gradient text, no glass effects.
4. **Desktop-dense, mobile-decisive** — desktop layouts can carry density and long-form thought; mobile should cut to the chase (framework summary + contact).
5. **Spanish-first, industrial-Spanish** — copy style is operational jargon (RFQ, ERP, AFIP/SENASA). Don't translate to softer consumer language.
